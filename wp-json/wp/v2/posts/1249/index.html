Mohun Bagan Super Giant on Friday appointed its former head coach Antonio Lopez Habas of Spain as the team\u2019s new Technical Director ahead of the upcoming Indian Super League season.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Habas returns to India, having previously managed ATK Mohun Bagan during the Hero ISL 2021-22 season.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u201cAlong with making a powerful first-team side, Mohun Bagan Super Giant wants to stress on youth development with the aim of producing first-team players from the various age-group sides.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\u201cHabas will be in charge of technical instructions for all the sides. The development side is currently training for the Calcutta League,\u201d the club said in a statement.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The Spaniard is the first head coach to win two Hero ISL titles. He began his journey with Atletico de\u00a0Kolkata\u00a0during the 2014 edition and became the first head coach to win the ISL title.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Habas had a stint with FC\u00a0Pune\u00a0City later on before returning to India to join ATK FC, leading them to the Hero ISL title during the 2019-20 season.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

He took charge of ATK Mohun Bagan the following season and guided them to the ISL final that year. He parted ways with the Mariners midway during the 2021-22 season.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Dias returns to Jamshedpur FC after a successful coaching stint as head coach with Ambernath United Atlanta FC.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Under Dias, Ambernath United reached the final round of the Hero I-League Second Division this year, only narrowly missing out on becoming champions of the I-League Second Division where they needed just a draw against\u00a0Delhi\u00a0FC.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

He led the club to successive undefeated\u00a0Mumbai\u00a0FA Elite Division titles in 2022 and 2023 as well as back-to-back Mumbai Women\u2019s League titles.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

He was also the head coach of the Maharashtra Santosh Trophy team, leading them to the final round.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
